"History"

White water rafting has a rich history that dates back to the mid-19th century. The sport originated in the United States, particularly in the western regions where rivers with challenging rapids were abundant. Early explorers and adventurers would navigate these rivers using makeshift rafts made from logs or inflated animal skins. The modern sport of white water rafting began to take shape in the 1950s and 1960s when inflatable rafts became more widely available. This innovation allowed for greater maneuverability and safety on the water, making it accessible to a broader audience. The first commercial rafting trips were organized in the Grand Canyon in the late 1950s, attracting thrill-seekers and outdoor enthusiasts. Over the years, white water rafting has grown in popularity worldwide, with numerous rivers across North America, Europe, Asia, and other continents offering exciting rafting opportunities. Today, it is a well- established adventure sport that combines adrenaline-pumping excitement with the beauty of nature, drawing millions of participants each year.
